# Earcon Explorer

Earcons are short audio tones that JJ Flexible Radio Access plays to confirm actions and to communicate state changes. They are designed to be quick and informative, without interrupting your workflow the way a full spoken announcement might.

## What Earcons Are

Think of earcons as audio icons. Instead of a visual checkmark or a colour change on the screen, you hear a brief tone. A rising tone generally means "on" or "success"; a falling tone means "off" or "cancel"; a buzz means "error" or "unrecognised."

## Common Earcons You Will Hear

- **Rising tone (bink)** — feature turned on, or action confirmed.
- **Falling tone (bonk)** — feature turned off, or cancelled.
- **Rising two-step (bonk-bink)** — a DSP feature toggled on via the leader key.
- **Falling two-step (bink-bonk)** — a DSP feature toggled off via the leader key.
- **Soft descending tone** — a leader-key timeout or an Escape cancellation.
- **Dull buzz** — an invalid key was pressed, or the command was not recognised in the current context.
- **Double chime** — help was requested (pressing `?` after the leader key).
- **Chirp** — the tune carrier was toggled on or off.
- **Pulsing tone** — an ATU tune cycle is in progress.
- **Rising arpeggio** — the ATU tune succeeded.
- **Falling arpeggio** — the ATU tune failed because no match was found.

## Earcons and Screen Readers

Earcons play alongside your screen reader, not instead of it. After a toggle earcon plays, the application also speaks the state change — for example, "Noise Reduction on." The earcon gives you instant feedback, while the speech provides the full confirmation of what changed.

## Customising Earcons

The Earcon Explorer dialog lets you preview and customise earcons. Open it by going to the **Help** menu and choosing **Earcon Explorer**. Inside the Explorer you can listen to each earcon in isolation, so you can learn what each one sounds like before you hear it fire in the middle of a QSO.
