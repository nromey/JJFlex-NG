# Frequency Entry Sounds

When you type digits into the frequency field, JJ Flex plays a short sound per keystroke. It confirms the digit registered without you having to wait for the screen reader to catch up, and it gives the whole experience a tactile feel — less "typing into the void," more like a keypad with feedback.

## The built-in sound modes

Settings > Audio > Frequency Entry Sound offers these modes:

- Musical notes — the keys map to different pitches. Pleasant and varied; different digits sound meaningfully different so after a while you hear the number as a phrase.
- Single tone — every key plays the same short pitch. Minimal, consistent, doesn't distract.
- Random tones — a different random-ish pitch per keystroke within a pleasant range. Adds subtle variety without being patterned.
- Off — silent typing, same as any other text field. If you find sounds distracting during a contest or while you're already flooded with speech output, turn this off.

## Choosing a mode

Entirely personal. A lot of ops run Musical notes because the pitch mapping becomes its own mental shortcut — after a week you can hear "14.225" without even thinking about it. Others prefer Single tone because it stays out of the way during busy operating. There's no right answer.

## Changing modes mid-session

Change it in Settings at any time. The new mode takes effect on the next keystroke. You can also turn it off for a session and back on later without losing any other settings.

## Hidden modes

A few alternative sound modes are tucked away in the app that aren't in the default list. They're intentionally undocumented. If you find them, enjoy — they're not secret for any particular reason beyond being a little treat for people who poke around. If you don't find them, you haven't missed anything functional; the documented modes cover the actual use cases.
