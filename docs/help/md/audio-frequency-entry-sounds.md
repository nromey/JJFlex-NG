# Frequency Entry Sounds

When you type digits into the frequency field, JJ Flexible Radio Access plays a short sound for each keystroke. The sound confirms that the digit registered without you having to wait for the screen reader to catch up, and it gives the whole experience a tactile feel — less "typing into the void," more like a keypad with real feedback.

## The Built-in Sound Modes

Under **Settings > Audio > Frequency Entry Sound** you will find these modes to choose from:

- **Musical notes** — the digit keys map to different pitches. Pleasant and varied; different digits sound meaningfully different, so after a while you can hear the number as a short musical phrase.
- **Single tone** — every digit key plays the same short pitch. Minimal, consistent, and stays out of the way.
- **Random tones** — a different random-ish pitch plays for each keystroke within a pleasant range. Adds subtle variety without being patterned.
- **Off** — silent typing, the same behaviour as any other text field. If you find the sounds distracting during a contest, or if you are already flooded with speech output, turn this off.

## Choosing a Mode

The choice is entirely personal. A lot of operators run the Musical notes mode because the pitch mapping becomes its own mental shortcut — after a week, you can hear "14.225" without even thinking about it. Others prefer the Single tone mode because it stays quieter during busy operating. There is no right answer.

## Changing Modes During a Session

You can change the Frequency Entry Sound mode in Settings at any time. The new mode takes effect on the next keystroke, and you can toggle it off for a session and back on later without losing any other audio settings.

## Hidden Modes

A few alternative sound modes are tucked away in the application that do not appear in the default list. They are intentionally undocumented. If you stumble across them, enjoy — they are not secret for any particular reason beyond being a little treat for people who poke around. If you do not find them, you have not missed anything functional; the documented modes cover the actual use cases.
