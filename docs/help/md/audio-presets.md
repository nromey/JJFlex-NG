# Audio Presets

Audio presets let you save a complete audio configuration — levels, routing, and equalization — and recall it later with a single action.

## What Gets Saved in a Preset

An audio preset captures your current audio settings, including gain levels, headphone and line-out volumes, and your routing configuration. When you load the preset, the application restores all of those settings together, so you do not have to adjust them one at a time.

## Using Presets

You manage audio presets from inside the Audio Workshop. To open the Audio Workshop, press `Ctrl+Shift+W` from anywhere in the application. Once the Audio Workshop is open, you can:

- **Save** your current audio settings as a new named preset.
- **Load** a previously saved preset to restore those settings to the radio.
- **Delete** presets you no longer need.

## When to Use Presets

Presets are handy when your ideal audio setup changes depending on what you are doing on the air. A few common examples:

- **Ragchewing** — comfortable audio levels, perhaps a little noise reduction.
- **Contesting** — tighter filters and a different headphone balance for long listening sessions.
- **DX chasing** — higher gain and more aggressive noise reduction to pull weak signals out of the noise floor.
- **CW operation** — a narrow filter, the Audio Peak Filter enabled, and potentially different audio routing.

Save each setup as its own preset, and switching between operating styles becomes one load-action away.
