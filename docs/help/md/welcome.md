# Welcome to JJ Flexible Radio Access

JJ Flexible Radio Access (JJFlexRadio) is a Windows desktop application for controlling FlexRadio FLEX-6000 and FLEX-8000 series transceivers. It was built from the ground up to be fully accessible with screen readers like NVDA and JAWS.

Whether you're working a pileup on 20 meters or just ragchewing on 80, JJ Flexible Radio Access gives you complete keyboard control of your Flex radio with clear audio feedback at every step.

## What's New

Recent releases have added a bunch of new features:

- **Meter Sonification** — A full meters panel with up to 8 simultaneous audio tones mapped to S-meter, power, ALC, SWR, and more. Configurable waveforms, stereo panning, and presets (RX Monitor, TX Monitor, Full Monitor).
- **Audio Workshop** — One-stop control for audio routing, levels, and TX bandwidth sculpting. Open it with Ctrl+Shift+W or the leader key.
- **Leader Key (Ctrl+J)** — Quick access to DSP toggles, meter controls, and status commands. Press Ctrl+J then a single letter.
- **TX Sculpting** — Shape your transmitted audio by adjusting TX filter edges from the keyboard with Ctrl+Shift+[ ] and Ctrl+Alt+[ ] keys.
- **60-Meter Channels** — Navigate US 60m channels with Alt+Shift+Up/Down. The app speaks channel names and frequencies.
- **Antenna Switching** — Antenna ScreenFields category (Ctrl+Shift+5) for quick antenna and ATU control.
- **Tune Carrier & ATU** — Ctrl+Shift+T toggles tune carrier, Ctrl+T starts an ATU tune cycle with audio feedback.
- **Slice Management** — Multi-slice aware status, improved slice switching and announcements.
- **Command Finder** — Press Ctrl+Shift+H (or Ctrl+/) to search for any command by name.
- **Startup Speech** — The app greets you on launch and speaks your radio's model, frequency, mode, and band when you connect.

## Getting Help Quickly

Press `Ctrl+Shift+H` at any time to open the Command Finder. It lets you search every command by name. Just start typing what you want to do. You can also press `Ctrl+/` to open it.

Press `F1` from anywhere to open this help file. If you're focused on a specific part of the app (like the filters panel or the log), F1 will jump straight to the relevant help page.
