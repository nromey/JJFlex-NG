Rollback of yesterday's vendor library upgrade
==============================================

Hey Don,

Thank you for sending the trace this morning. That was exactly what we
needed to see what went wrong. Your radio connecting but no frequency
showing up — and the app feeling unresponsive — those weren't general
problems with the new build. Looking at your trace told us a specific
story about a specific thing.

What we found: the new vendor library underneath JJ Flex (FlexLib 4.2.18)
behaves differently on the LAN connect path than the older one did. The
radio gets the connect, the slice gets created, the audio plays — but
one little handshake message (the radio confirming back to JJ Flex what
its station name is now set to) doesn't come back through promptly on
the new library. So the app sits there waiting for that confirmation,
which is the "stuck on connecting" feel you saw.

What we found out comparing earlier traces: this is specifically a
mismatch between the new library and the firmware version your radio is
currently running. Older firmware doesn't speak the new dialect cleanly
on the LAN path. The earlier successful test we did was over SmartLink,
which uses different plumbing, so it didn't surface there.

What this build does: it rolls back yesterday's vendor library bump.
You should be back to the same behavior you had two days ago. Same
connect speed, same frequency display, same audio. Nothing fancy here --
just back to known good while we work on the proper fix.

What the proper fix looks like: we're going to build an in-app firmware
update feature. Once that's ready, JJ Flex will offer to update your
radio's firmware to a version that speaks the new library's dialect. At
that point we can put the new library back and you'll get all of its
improvements without the LAN handshake issue.

What to test
------------

Nothing unusual — just see if your normal day works:

1. Open JJ Flex. It should auto-connect like before.
2. Check that the frequency reads out correctly when you tab to it.
3. Listen — audio should be clean.
4. Try cycling slices, changing bands, basic operation.
5. Disconnect and reconnect a few times if you want.

If anything weird happens, drop a trace into your Dropbox folder same
as before. If everything feels normal, that's the answer we're hoping
for and you don't have to do anything.

One thing worth knowing for next time: if you ever hit "Connecting..."
and it feels stuck, you can press Escape now and the modal will cancel
cleanly — no taskkill needed. That shipped a couple of weeks ago in the
nightly stream but you may not have had reason to discover it.

Thanks again for the fast trace turnaround. Your testing is what made
this a same-day catch instead of something we shipped publicly.
